Severe Toothache / Deep Cavity

Persistent, throbbing pain or sharp reaction to cold/hot liquids often points to bacterial decay reaching the inner tooth nerve. This requires timely treatment to prevent serious infections or abscesses.

Immediate Self-Care Tips:
  • Rinse your mouth with warm salt water.
  • Avoid chewing on the painful side.
  • Book a dental examination as soon as possible.
Suggested: Orthodontics Braces

Teeth Alignment & Crowding

Misaligned teeth, overcrowding, or gap issues are highly common and can cause plaque accumulation, uneven tooth wear, and chewing difficulties. Braces or aligners help achieve a balanced bite.

Immediate Self-Care Tips:
  • Use dental floss regularly to clean tight gaps.
  • Avoid opening bottles or hard objects with your teeth.
  • Schedule an orthodontic checkup to explore braces options.
Suggested: Ceramic Crowns / Bridges

Chipped, Missing or Broken Teeth

A broken, chipped, or missing tooth compromises your chewing efficiency and causes neighboring teeth to drift. Custom ceramic crowns or bridges restore function and aesthetic symmetry.

Immediate Self-Care Tips:
  • Keep the area clean by gentle rinsing.
  • If a tooth chipped recently, try to locate and save the fragment.
  • Schedule a crown/bridge restoration consultation.
Suggested: Scaling & Cleaning

Yellowing, Stains or Plaque

Stains from food, tea, or smoking, alongside tartar accumulation, can lead to gum infections and enamel decay. Professional scaling and polishing safely restores your natural brightness.

Immediate Self-Care Tips:
  • Brush twice daily with a soft-bristled brush.
  • Rinse with water immediately after drinking coffee or tea.
  • Get a professional scaling and teeth cleaning done twice a year.
Suggested: Periodontal / Gum Care

Bleeding Gums & Bad Breath

Bleeding gums during brushing or persistent bad breath are signs of gingivitis (gum inflammation). If ignored, plaque hardens below the gumline and can lead to tooth loosening.

Immediate Self-Care Tips:
  • Floss daily to remove interdental plaque.
  • Use a mild antiseptic mouthwash.
  • Schedule a cleaning and gum checkup immediately.
Suggested: Emergency Care

Acute Pain or Dental Trauma

Severe, unbearable toothache, facial swelling, or a knocked-out tooth from physical trauma are clinical emergencies. Quick attention can save a tooth and prevent serious systemic infections.

Emergency Instructions:
  • If a tooth is knocked out, keep it moist (in milk or saliva) and seek care within 1 hour.
  • Apply a cold compress outside the cheek to reduce swelling.
  • Contact the clinic immediately for an urgent slot.
COMMON PATIENT QUERIES

Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about treatments, pain management, or booking procedures? We have compiled direct answers for you.

Is root canal treatment (RCT) painful?

No, modern Root Canal Treatments are virtually pain-free. We use advanced local anesthesia to completely numb the tooth and surrounding area before the procedure. Most patients report that the procedure feels no different than getting a standard deep filling, and it immediately relieves the severe toothache caused by the infection.

How long does teeth straightening (braces) treatment take?

The duration of orthodontic treatment varies depending on the severity of misalignment, crowding, or gaps. On average, active treatment with dental braces or clear aligners takes between 12 to 24 months. During this time, you will visit the clinic once every 4 to 6 weeks for adjustments and progress monitoring.

What sterilization protocols do you follow?

Patient safety is our absolute priority. We strictly follow WHO and IDA sterilization guidelines. All reusable dental instruments undergo a rigorous 4-step process: chemical pre-disinfection, ultrasonic cleaning, pouching, and high-pressure steam sterilization in a medical-grade autoclave. Single-use items are discarded immediately, and treatment chairs are sanitized after every patient.

Do I need to book an appointment in advance?

While we do accommodate walk-in patients when slots are free, we highly recommend booking an appointment in advance. Scheduling your visit ensures minimal waiting times and allows Dr. Shailendra Kumar to dedicate full, uninterrupted time to your diagnosis and treatment. You can use our online form or call us to reserve a slot.

What should I do in case of a dental emergency?

In case of a dental emergency (such as a knocked-out tooth, severe jaw trauma, facial swelling, or bleeding), contact the clinic immediately. If a tooth is knocked out, handle it only by the crown (top part), rinse it gently without scrubbing, and place it in a container of cold milk or clean saliva. Seek emergency dental care within 60 minutes for the highest chance of saving the tooth.
